=== DIETARY RECOMMENDATIONS AND SPECIAL DIETS GUIDE ===

--- VEGETARIAN DIET ---
Definition: Excludes meat, poultry, and seafood. May include dairy and eggs.

Types:
- Lacto-vegetarian: Includes dairy, excludes eggs (most common in India)
- Ovo-vegetarian: Includes eggs, excludes dairy
- Lacto-ovo-vegetarian: Includes both dairy and eggs

Protein sources for vegetarians:
- Dairy: Paneer, milk, yogurt, cheese (high calcium + protein)
- Eggs: Complete protein source
- Legumes: Lentils, chickpeas, rajma, chana dal
- Nuts and seeds: Almonds, peanuts, flaxseeds
- Whole grains: Quinoa (complete protein), amaranth

Nutrients to watch:
- Vitamin B12: Limited to dairy and eggs
- Iron: Use with vitamin C for better absorption
- Zinc: Legumes, nuts, seeds
- Omega-3: Flaxseeds, walnuts, chia seeds

Sample high-protein vegetarian meals:
- Breakfast: Paneer bhurji with whole wheat toast + milk
- Lunch: Rajma rice with yogurt raita
- Dinner: Dal tadka + roti + vegetable sabzi

--- VEGAN DIET ---
Definition: Excludes all animal products including dairy, eggs, honey

Critical nutrients to supplement:
1. Vitamin B12 (ESSENTIAL supplement — not found in plant foods)
2. Vitamin D3 (vegan: D2 or algae-based D3)
3. Omega-3 (algae oil)
4. Iron (legumes + vitamin C)
5. Calcium (fortified plant milks, tofu, leafy greens)
6. Zinc (legumes, seeds, fortified foods)
7. Iodine (iodized salt, seaweed)

Top plant-based protein foods:
- Tofu: 8g protein/100g
- Tempeh: 19g protein/100g
- Edamame: 11g protein/100g
- Lentils: 9g protein/100g cooked
- Black beans: 9g protein/100g cooked
- Quinoa: 4g protein/100g cooked (complete protein)
- Seitan (wheat gluten): 25g protein/100g

Vegan Indian recipes: Most dal dishes are naturally vegan (skip ghee, use oil). Vegetable biryanis, aloo matar, chana masala, baingan bharta are naturally vegan.

--- JAIN DIET ---
Definition: Strict vegetarian diet avoiding root vegetables and some other foods for religious/ethical reasons

STRICTLY AVOIDED:
- Root vegetables: Onion, garlic, potato, carrot, beetroot, radish, turnip, leeks
- Eggplant/brinjal (some Jains)
- Consumption restricted during certain periods (Paryushana)

Reason: Root vegetables are believed to contain many microorganisms; pulling them harms the entire plant

ALLOWED:
- Above-ground vegetables: Tomatoes, peas, beans, capsicum, okra, pumpkin
- Leafy vegetables: Spinach, fenugreek leaves
- Fruits
- Dairy products
- Grains, legumes, nuts

Jain cooking adaptations:
- Replace onion with asafoetida (hing) and fenugreek leaves
- Replace garlic with ginger + hing
- Use raw banana or yam instead of potato
- Use hing generously as flavor base

Popular Jain recipes:
- Jain Dal Makhani (without onion/garlic)
- Jain Khichdi
- Jain Kadhi
- Panchkutiyu Dal (Gujarati Jain specialty)

--- GLUTEN-FREE DIET ---
Definition: Eliminates gluten, a protein found in wheat, barley, rye

Foods to STRICTLY AVOID:
- Wheat flour (maida, atta)
- Barley (jau)
- Rye
- Most bread, pasta, noodles
- Soy sauce (contains wheat)
- Beer (made from barley)

Hidden sources of gluten:
- Sauces (soy sauce, teriyaki)
- Soups (thickened with flour)
- Processed foods
- Oats (unless labeled gluten-free — cross-contamination risk)

Safe gluten-free grains and alternatives:
- Rice and rice flour
- Corn and cornstarch
- Quinoa
- Millet (bajra, jowar, ragi)
- Buckwheat (no relation to wheat despite name)
- Chickpea flour (besan)
- Almond flour, coconut flour

Naturally gluten-free Indian dishes:
- All dal recipes (using natural dal, no additives)
- Most rice preparations
- Sabzis (vegetable dishes without flour)
- Tandoori dishes (watch marinade)
- Dosa, idli (rice-based)
- Ragi roti

Note for Celiac patients: Even small amounts of gluten cause intestinal damage. Dedicated utensils and cooking surfaces needed.

--- DIABETIC-FRIENDLY DIET ---
Goal: Stable blood sugar levels through low-GI foods

Key principles:
1. Choose low-GI foods (GI < 55)
2. Include fiber at every meal
3. Eat smaller, frequent meals
4. Limit refined carbs and sugars
5. Pair carbs with protein and healthy fats

Low-GI food choices:
- Grains: Oats (GI 55), brown rice (GI 50), barley (GI 28)
- Legumes: Lentils (GI 32), chickpeas (GI 28), rajma (GI 29) — excellent choices
- Vegetables: Most non-starchy vegetables (GI 15-35)
- Fruits: Berries, guava, papaya, apple (avoid mangoes, bananas in large amounts)

Foods to limit:
- White rice, white bread, maida
- Sugary beverages, fruit juices
- Sweet snacks, mithai
- Processed foods

Sample diabetic-friendly Indian menu:
- Breakfast: Vegetable poha + 1 boiled egg + buttermilk
- Lunch: 2 multigrain rotis + dal + mixed vegetable sabzi + salad
- Dinner: 1 bowl daliya khichdi + curd
- Snacks: Roasted chana, fresh fruit (small portion)

--- HIGH-PROTEIN DIET ---
Goal: 25-35% of calories from protein

Benefits: Muscle building, satiety, metabolic health

Daily targets:
- Athletes/muscle building: 1.6-2.2g protein per kg body weight
- Weight management: 1.2-1.6g per kg
- General health: 0.8-1.0g per kg

High-protein vegetarian meal plan:
- Breakfast: Greek yogurt with nuts and seeds + eggs (if not vegan) — 25g protein
- Lunch: Rajma/chickpea curry + paneer sabzi + 1 roti — 35g protein
- Dinner: Tofu stir-fry + quinoa — 30g protein
- Snacks: Roasted edamame, boiled eggs, paneer cubes — 15g protein

Protein combining tip: Grains + legumes together = complete protein (dal + rice, roti + dal)

--- LOW-CARB DIET ---
Goal: Limit carbohydrates to 50-150g/day

Focus foods:
- Proteins: All meats, eggs, paneer, Greek yogurt
- Fats: Nuts, seeds, olive oil, avocado, ghee
- Low-carb vegetables: Leafy greens, broccoli, cauliflower, capsicum, zucchini, mushrooms

Foods to avoid:
- Grains (rice, roti, bread, pasta)
- Root vegetables in large quantities
- Sugary fruits
- Legumes (moderate amounts only)

Low-carb Indian adaptations:
- Cauliflower rice instead of rice
- Paneer scramble instead of dal-rice
- Vegetable curries without potato
- Egg dishes
- Tandoori items (skip bread)

--- WEIGHT MANAGEMENT ---
Caloric deficit for weight loss: 300-500 calories below maintenance
Caloric surplus for weight gain: 200-400 calories above maintenance

Weight loss tips in Indian cooking:
- Use less oil (1-2 tsp instead of 4-6 tbsp)
- Prefer baking/roasting/air-frying over deep frying
- Increase vegetable portion, decrease grain portion
- Use full-fat dairy in moderation for satiety
- Choose whole grains over refined

--- HEART-HEALTHY DIET ---
Guidelines:
- Limit saturated fat, eliminate trans fats
- Increase fiber (target 25-35g daily)
- Reduce sodium (less than 2300mg daily)
- Increase omega-3 fatty acids
- Moderate alcohol consumption

Heart-healthy Indian cooking:
- Replace ghee with olive oil or minimal amounts
- Steam or boil instead of frying
- Use less salt; flavor with spices and lemon
- Include walnuts, flaxseeds daily
- Garlic, onions, turmeric have heart-protective properties
- Oats, lentils, beans reduce LDL cholesterol

--- POST-WORKOUT NUTRITION ---
Within 30-60 minutes after exercise:
- Protein: 20-40g for muscle repair
- Carbs: 1g per kg body weight for glycogen replenishment
- Avoid high fat immediately post-workout

Quick Indian post-workout snacks:
- Paneer + roti (fast prep)
- Greek yogurt with banana
- Egg white omelet with whole wheat toast
- Chana chaat (chickpea salad)

--- IMMUNITY BOOSTING FOODS ---
Key nutrients and sources:
- Vitamin C: Lemon, guava, bell peppers, amla (Indian gooseberry)
- Zinc: Pumpkin seeds, legumes, nuts
- Turmeric: Curcumin has anti-inflammatory properties
- Ginger: Anti-inflammatory, digestive support
- Garlic: Allicin has antimicrobial properties
- Probiotics: Yogurt, curd, fermented foods (idli, dosa, kanji)

Immunity-boosting recipes:
- Golden milk (haldi doodh): Turmeric + warm milk + pepper + honey
- Ginger-tulsi kadha (traditional Indian remedy)
- Amla juice (high Vitamin C)
- Curd with meals (probiotic benefit)
