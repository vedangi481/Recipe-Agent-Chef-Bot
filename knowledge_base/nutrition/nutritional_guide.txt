=== NUTRITIONAL INFORMATION GUIDE ===

--- SECTION 1: MACRONUTRIENT OVERVIEW ---

PROTEINS:
- Essential for muscle repair, immune function, and enzyme production
- Recommended daily intake: 0.8g per kg body weight (sedentary), 1.2-2.0g/kg (active)
- High-protein vegetarian sources: Lentils (18g/100g dry), Chickpeas (19g/100g dry), Tofu (8g/100g), Paneer (18g/100g), Greek yogurt (10g/100g), Quinoa (14g/100g dry), Edamame (11g/100g)
- High-protein non-vegetarian sources: Eggs (13g/100g), Chicken breast (31g/100g), Fish (20-25g/100g), Cottage cheese (11g/100g)

CARBOHYDRATES:
- Primary energy source for the body and brain
- Complex carbs preferred over simple sugars
- High-fiber carb sources: Oats (10g fiber/100g dry), Lentils (8g fiber/100g cooked), Sweet potato (3g fiber/100g), Brown rice (2g fiber/100g cooked)
- Low-GI carbs (better for blood sugar): Lentils (GI 32), Chickpeas (GI 28), Sweet potato (GI 44), Oats (GI 55)
- High-GI carbs (quick energy): White bread (GI 75), White rice (GI 72), Watermelon (GI 76)

FATS:
- Essential for hormone production, vitamin absorption, and brain health
- Healthy unsaturated fats: Olive oil, avocado, nuts, seeds, fatty fish
- Saturated fats (limit): Butter, ghee, coconut oil, red meat
- Trans fats (avoid): Processed/packaged foods, partially hydrogenated oils
- Omega-3 sources: Flaxseeds, walnuts, chia seeds, fatty fish

--- SECTION 3: VITAMINS AND MINERALS ---

VITAMIN C (Immune support, iron absorption):
- Rich sources: Bell peppers (128mg/100g), Guava (228mg/100g), Lemon (53mg/100g), Tomatoes (23mg/100g)
- Daily requirement: 65-90mg

IRON (Oxygen transport, energy):
- Rich vegetarian sources: Spinach (2.7mg/100g), Lentils (3.3mg/100g), Tofu (3.4mg/100g), Pumpkin seeds (8.8mg/30g)
- Absorption tip: Eat with Vitamin C to enhance iron absorption
- Avoid: Tea/coffee immediately after iron-rich meals

CALCIUM (Bone health):
- Rich sources: Dairy products, tofu (if calcium-set), broccoli, fortified plant milks
- Milk (100ml): 113mg calcium
- Yogurt (100g): 110mg calcium

VITAMIN B12 (Nerve function — critical for vegans):
- Found ONLY in animal products naturally
- Vegetarian sources: Dairy, eggs
- Vegan sources: Fortified plant milks, nutritional yeast, B12 supplements

VITAMIN D (Bone health, immune function):
- Sunlight is primary source
- Food sources: Egg yolk, fortified foods, fatty fish
- Note: Deficiency very common in India

POTASSIUM (Heart health, blood pressure):
- Rich sources: Banana (358mg/100g), Potato (421mg/100g), Lentils (369mg/100g), Spinach (558mg/100g)

ZINC (Immune function, wound healing):
- Rich sources: Pumpkin seeds (7.6mg/30g), Chickpeas (1.5mg/100g cooked), Cashews (1.6mg/30g)

--- SECTION 4: DIETARY GUIDELINES ---

BALANCED PLATE METHOD:
- 1/2 plate: Non-starchy vegetables
- 1/4 plate: Whole grains/complex carbs
- 1/4 plate: Lean protein
- Add: Healthy fats, fruits, dairy

DAILY CALORIE GUIDELINES (approximate):
- Sedentary adult: 1,600-2,000 calories
- Active adult: 2,000-2,600 calories
- Children (6-12): 1,200-1,800 calories
- Elderly: 1,600-2,200 calories

HYDRATION:
- Daily water intake: 8-10 glasses (2-2.5 liters)
- Increase during exercise, hot weather
- Herbal teas, dal water, coconut water count toward intake

--- SECTION 5: FOOD SAFETY ---
- Cooked rice: Refrigerate within 2 hours; consume within 2 days
- Dal/legumes: Store 3-4 days refrigerated
- Cooked vegetables: Store 3-5 days
- Paneer: Use within 2-3 days of opening; freeze for longer storage
- Eggs: Refrigerate; use within 3-5 weeks
- Never leave cooked food at room temperature for more than 2 hours
- Wash hands before cooking and after handling raw produce
- Cook chicken/meat to internal temperature of 165°F/74°C
- Wash all vegetables and fruits before use

ALLERGEN WARNINGS:
Common allergens to declare: Milk, Eggs, Fish, Shellfish, Tree Nuts, Peanuts, Wheat (Gluten), Soybeans
- Cross-contamination risk: Use separate utensils for allergen-free cooking
- Always read labels on packaged products
- When cooking for others, always ask about allergies first
