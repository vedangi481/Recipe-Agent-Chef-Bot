--- RECIPE: Aloo Matar (Potato and Peas Curry) ---
Category: Vegetarian, Indian, Curry
Cuisine: North Indian
Prep Time: 15 minutes
Cook Time: 25 minutes
Total Time: 40 minutes
Servings: 4
Difficulty: Easy
Dietary: Vegetarian, Vegan (without ghee), Gluten-Free

Ingredients:
- 3 medium potatoes, peeled and cubed
- 1 cup green peas (fresh or frozen)
- 2 medium onions, finely chopped
- 2 tomatoes, pureed
- 2 tablespoons oil or ghee
- 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
- 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
- 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
- 1 teaspoon coriander powder
- 1 teaspoon cumin powder
- 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
- 1 teaspoon garam masala
- Salt to taste
- Fresh coriander leaves for garnish
- 1/2 cup water

Instructions:
1. Heat oil in a deep pan over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
2. Add chopped onions and sauté until golden brown (about 8-10 minutes).
3. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook for 2 minutes until raw smell disappears.
4. Add tomato puree and cook for 5 minutes until oil separates.
5. Add turmeric, coriander powder, cumin powder, and red chili powder. Mix well.
6. Add cubed potatoes and mix to coat with masala.
7. Add 1/2 cup water, cover, and cook on medium-low heat for 10 minutes.
8. Add green peas and cook for another 5-7 minutes until potatoes are tender.
9. Add garam masala and adjust salt.
10. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
11. Serve hot with roti, naan, or rice.

Nutrition (per serving): Calories: 180 | Protein: 5g | Carbs: 32g | Fat: 5g | Fiber: 6g
Tips: For a creamier curry, add 2 tablespoons of yogurt after the tomatoes. Mash a few potato pieces to thicken the gravy.

--- RECIPE: Paneer Butter Masala ---
Category: Vegetarian, Indian, Curry
Cuisine: North Indian, Restaurant Style
Prep Time: 20 minutes
Cook Time: 30 minutes
Total Time: 50 minutes
Servings: 4
Difficulty: Medium
Dietary: Vegetarian, Gluten-Free

Ingredients:
- 250g paneer (cottage cheese), cubed
- 2 large onions, roughly chopped
- 3 tomatoes, roughly chopped
- 2 tablespoons butter
- 1 tablespoon oil
- 1 tablespoon cashews
- 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
- 1 teaspoon red chili powder (Kashmiri for color)
- 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
- 1 teaspoon coriander powder
- 1 teaspoon cumin powder
- 1 teaspoon garam masala
- 1/2 cup fresh cream or heavy cream
- 1 teaspoon sugar
- Salt to taste
- 1 teaspoon kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves)
- 2 cardamom pods
- 2 cloves
- 1 bay leaf

Instructions:
1. Heat oil in a pan. Add whole spices (cardamom, cloves, bay leaf) and sauté briefly.
2. Add onions, tomatoes, and cashews. Cook until softened, about 10 minutes.
3. Add ginger-garlic paste, turmeric, red chili powder. Cook for 2 minutes.
4. Cool the mixture and blend into a smooth paste.
5. Strain the paste through a sieve for a silky texture.
6. Heat butter in the same pan. Pour in the strained paste.
7. Cook on medium heat, stirring frequently, for 5-7 minutes.
8. Add coriander powder, cumin powder, and sugar. Mix well.
9. Add 1/2 cup water to adjust consistency.
10. Gently add paneer cubes (fry them first for a firmer texture).
11. Add fresh cream and kasuri methi. Simmer for 5 minutes.
12. Add garam masala and adjust seasoning.
13. Serve with naan, roti, or jeera rice.

Nutrition (per serving): Calories: 380 | Protein: 14g | Carbs: 18g | Fat: 28g | Fiber: 3g
Tips: For restaurant-style color, use Kashmiri red chili. Fry paneer cubes in butter before adding to gravy for better texture.

--- RECIPE: Palak Paneer (Spinach Cottage Cheese) ---
Category: Vegetarian, Indian, Curry
Cuisine: North Indian
Prep Time: 15 minutes
Cook Time: 25 minutes
Total Time: 40 minutes
Servings: 4
Difficulty: Medium
Dietary: Vegetarian, Gluten-Free, High-Protein

Ingredients:
- 250g paneer, cubed
- 400g fresh spinach (palak), washed
- 1 onion, finely chopped
- 2 tomatoes, chopped
- 2 tablespoons oil
- 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
- 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
- 1 green chili
- 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
- 1 teaspoon coriander powder
- 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
- 2 tablespoons fresh cream (optional)
- Salt to taste

Instructions:
1. Blanch spinach in boiling water for 2 minutes. Transfer to ice water to retain color.
2. Blend blanched spinach into a smooth puree.
3. Heat oil, add cumin seeds, then onions. Sauté until golden.
4. Add ginger-garlic paste and green chili. Cook 2 minutes.
5. Add tomatoes, turmeric, and coriander powder. Cook until oil separates.
6. Add spinach puree and simmer for 5 minutes.
7. Gently add paneer cubes. Simmer 3-4 minutes.
8. Add garam masala and cream if using.
9. Serve with roti, naan, or paratha.

Nutrition (per serving): Calories: 280 | Protein: 16g | Carbs: 12g | Fat: 19g | Fiber: 4g | Iron: 8mg
Tips: Blanching spinach preserves its vibrant green color. Add a pinch of nutmeg for depth of flavor.

--- RECIPE: Masoor Dal (Red Lentil Soup) ---
Category: Vegan, Indian, Lentils
Cuisine: Indian
Prep Time: 5 minutes
Cook Time: 20 minutes
Total Time: 25 minutes
Servings: 4
Difficulty: Easy
Dietary: Vegan, Vegetarian, Gluten-Free, High-Protein, Diabetic-Friendly

Ingredients:
- 1 cup red lentils (masoor dal), rinsed
- 1 onion, finely chopped
- 2 tomatoes, chopped
- 2 tablespoons oil
- 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
- 1/2 teaspoon mustard seeds
- 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
- 1 teaspoon coriander powder
- 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
- 1 teaspoon garam masala
- 3 cups water
- Salt to taste
- Fresh coriander for garnish
- 1 lemon, juiced

Instructions:
1. Cook lentils in 3 cups water with turmeric until soft (about 15 minutes or pressure cook 3 whistles).
2. Heat oil in separate pan. Add mustard seeds and cumin seeds.
3. Add onions and cook until golden brown.
4. Add tomatoes and spices. Cook until oil separates.
5. Pour tempered masala into cooked dal.
6. Simmer together for 5 minutes.
7. Add lemon juice and garnish with coriander.
8. Serve with rice or roti.

Nutrition (per serving): Calories: 220 | Protein: 13g | Carbs: 35g | Fat: 5g | Fiber: 8g | Iron: 6mg
Tips: Perfect one-pot meal. Add spinach for extra iron and nutrients.

--- RECIPE: Vegetable Biryani ---
Category: Vegetarian, Rice, Indian
Cuisine: Hyderabadi, Mughal
Prep Time: 30 minutes
Cook Time: 45 minutes
Total Time: 75 minutes
Servings: 6
Difficulty: Hard
Dietary: Vegetarian, Can be Vegan

Ingredients:
- 2 cups basmati rice, soaked 30 minutes
- 2 cups mixed vegetables (carrots, peas, beans, potatoes)
- 2 onions, thinly sliced (for frying)
- 1 cup yogurt
- 3 tablespoons ghee or oil
- Whole spices: 2 bay leaves, 4 cardamom, 4 cloves, 1 cinnamon stick
- 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
- 1 teaspoon red chili powder
- 1 teaspoon biryani masala
- 1/2 teaspoon turmeric
- Saffron soaked in warm milk
- Fresh mint and coriander leaves
- Fried onions (barista)
- Salt to taste

Instructions:
1. Cook rice until 70% done with whole spices and salt.
2. Fry onions until golden brown and crispy. Set aside.
3. Marinate vegetables in yogurt, spices, and half the fried onions for 20 minutes.
4. Heat ghee in a heavy pot. Add marinated vegetables and cook 10 minutes.
5. Layer partially cooked rice over the vegetables.
6. Sprinkle saffron milk, mint, coriander, and remaining fried onions.
7. Seal the pot (dum cooking) and cook on very low heat for 20 minutes.
8. Gently mix before serving.

Nutrition (per serving): Calories: 420 | Protein: 9g | Carbs: 72g | Fat: 11g | Fiber: 5g
Tips: The secret to perfect biryani is the dum (steam cooking). Use a heavy-bottomed pot and seal with dough or foil.

--- RECIPE: Egg Bhurji (Indian Scrambled Eggs) ---
Category: Vegetarian, Eggs, Indian, Quick
Cuisine: Indian Street Food
Prep Time: 5 minutes
Cook Time: 10 minutes
Total Time: 15 minutes
Servings: 2
Difficulty: Easy
Dietary: Vegetarian, Gluten-Free, High-Protein

Ingredients:
- 4 eggs
- 1 onion, finely chopped
- 2 tomatoes, finely chopped
- 2 green chilies, chopped
- 2 tablespoons oil or butter
- 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
- 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
- 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
- 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
- Salt to taste
- Fresh coriander for garnish

Instructions:
1. Heat oil in a pan. Add cumin seeds.
2. Add onions and cook until soft and translucent.
3. Add green chilies and cook 1 minute.
4. Add tomatoes, turmeric, and red chili powder. Cook until tomatoes are soft.
5. Beat eggs and pour into the pan.
6. Scramble continuously on medium heat.
7. Add garam masala and salt.
8. Garnish with coriander. Serve with toast or pav.

Nutrition (per serving): Calories: 260 | Protein: 18g | Carbs: 8g | Fat: 18g
Tips: For street-style taste, add a tablespoon of butter at the end. Don't overcook - eggs should be creamy.

--- RECIPE: Khichdi (Rice and Lentil Porridge) ---
Category: Vegetarian, Comfort Food, Indian
Cuisine: Indian
Prep Time: 10 minutes
Cook Time: 20 minutes
Total Time: 30 minutes
Servings: 4
Difficulty: Easy
Dietary: Vegetarian, Vegan (without ghee), Gluten-Free, Diabetic-Friendly

Ingredients:
- 1 cup rice
- 1/2 cup yellow moong dal
- 1 onion, chopped
- 1 tomato, chopped
- 2 tablespoons ghee
- 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
- 1/2 teaspoon turmeric
- 1/2 teaspoon ginger, grated
- 4 cups water
- Salt to taste
- Fresh coriander

Instructions:
1. Wash rice and dal together. Soak for 15 minutes.
2. Heat ghee in pressure cooker. Add cumin seeds.
3. Add onion and cook until soft.
4. Add ginger and tomato. Cook 3 minutes.
5. Add turmeric and drained rice-dal mixture.
6. Add 4 cups water and salt.
7. Pressure cook for 3 whistles or until soft.
8. Garnish with ghee and coriander.

Nutrition (per serving): Calories: 280 | Protein: 10g | Carbs: 48g | Fat: 6g | Fiber: 4g
Tips: Perfect sick-day comfort food. Adjust water for desired consistency. Great base for baby food.

--- RECIPE: Tomato Rice (Thakkali Sadam) ---
Category: Vegan, Rice, South Indian
Cuisine: South Indian (Tamil Nadu)
Prep Time: 10 minutes
Cook Time: 20 minutes
Total Time: 30 minutes
Servings: 4
Difficulty: Easy
Dietary: Vegan, Vegetarian, Gluten-Free

Ingredients:
- 2 cups cooked rice (preferably day-old)
- 3 tomatoes, finely chopped
- 1 onion, finely chopped
- 2 tablespoons oil
- 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
- 1 teaspoon chana dal
- 1 teaspoon urad dal
- Few curry leaves
- 2 dried red chilies
- 1/2 teaspoon turmeric
- 1 teaspoon sambar powder or rasam powder
- Salt to taste
- Fresh coriander for garnish

Instructions:
1. Heat oil in a pan. Add mustard seeds - let them splutter.
2. Add chana dal, urad dal, and dried red chilies. Fry until golden.
3. Add curry leaves and onions. Cook until onions are soft.
4. Add tomatoes, turmeric, and sambar powder. Cook until tomatoes are mushy.
5. Add cooked rice and mix gently to coat.
6. Season with salt and garnish with coriander.
7. Serve with papad, raita, or pickle.

Nutrition (per serving): Calories: 240 | Protein: 5g | Carbs: 48g | Fat: 5g
Tips: Great way to use leftover rice. Add peanuts for extra crunch and protein.

--- RECIPE: Bread Upma ---
Category: Vegetarian, Breakfast, Quick
Cuisine: Indian
Prep Time: 5 minutes
Cook Time: 10 minutes
Total Time: 15 minutes
Servings: 2
Difficulty: Easy
Dietary: Vegetarian

Ingredients:
- 4 slices bread, cubed
- 1 onion, chopped
- 1 tomato, chopped
- 1 green chili
- 2 tablespoons oil
- 1/2 teaspoon mustard seeds
- Few curry leaves
- 1/4 teaspoon turmeric
- Salt and pepper
- Lemon juice

Instructions:
1. Heat oil, add mustard seeds and curry leaves.
2. Add onion and green chili, cook until soft.
3. Add tomato and turmeric, cook 3 minutes.
4. Add bread pieces and toss to coat with masala.
5. Season with salt and lemon juice.
6. Serve hot with chutney.

Nutrition (per serving): Calories: 220 | Protein: 6g | Carbs: 32g | Fat: 8g
Tips: Perfect for using stale bread. Add vegetables like peas or corn for variety.

--- RECIPE: Aloo Paratha ---
Category: Vegetarian, Breakfast, Indian Bread
Cuisine: Punjabi
Prep Time: 30 minutes
Cook Time: 20 minutes
Total Time: 50 minutes
Servings: 4 (8 parathas)
Difficulty: Medium
Dietary: Vegetarian

Ingredients (Dough):
- 2 cups whole wheat flour
- Water as needed
- 1/2 teaspoon salt
- 1 tablespoon oil

Ingredients (Filling):
- 4 medium potatoes, boiled and mashed
- 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
- 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
- 1 teaspoon garam masala
- 1/2 teaspoon amchur (dry mango powder)
- Fresh coriander, chopped
- Salt to taste
- Green chilies (optional)

Instructions:
1. Make soft dough with flour, water, and oil. Rest 30 minutes.
2. Mix mashed potatoes with all filling spices.
3. Divide dough and filling into equal portions.
4. Roll dough ball, place filling in center, fold and seal.
5. Gently roll into a flat round paratha.
6. Cook on hot griddle with butter or ghee on both sides until golden.
7. Serve with yogurt, pickle, and butter.

Nutrition (per paratha): Calories: 180 | Protein: 5g | Carbs: 30g | Fat: 5g | Fiber: 4g
Tips: Knead dough well for soft parathas. Ensure filling is at room temperature before stuffing.
