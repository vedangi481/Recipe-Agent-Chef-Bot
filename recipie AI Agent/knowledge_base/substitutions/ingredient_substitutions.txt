=== INGREDIENT SUBSTITUTION GUIDE ===

--- EGGS SUBSTITUTIONS ---
Use Case: Binding in baked goods
- 1 egg = 3 tablespoons aquafaba (chickpea liquid) — Best for meringues and macarons
- 1 egg = 1 tablespoon ground flaxseed + 3 tablespoons water (flax egg) — Best for cookies, muffins
- 1 egg = 1 tablespoon chia seeds + 3 tablespoons water — Best for baked goods
- 1 egg = 1/4 cup unsweetened applesauce — Adds moisture, subtle sweetness
- 1 egg = 1/4 cup mashed banana — Adds banana flavor
- 1 egg = 3 tablespoons nut butter (peanut/almond) — Adds protein
Reason: These substitutes mimic the binding and moisture properties of eggs.

--- BUTTER SUBSTITUTIONS ---
- 1 cup butter = 1 cup coconut oil (solid) — Works well in baking, adds slight coconut flavor
- 1 cup butter = 3/4 cup olive oil — For savory dishes and sautéing
- 1 cup butter = 1 cup Greek yogurt — Reduces fat, adds protein
- 1 cup butter = 1 cup avocado (mashed) — Healthier, adds nutrients
- 1 cup butter = 3/4 cup vegetable/canola oil — Neutral flavor, good for baking
Reason: Oil-based substitutes work because butter is mostly fat. Reduce quantity by 25% when swapping butter for liquid oil.

--- MILK SUBSTITUTIONS ---
- 1 cup dairy milk = 1 cup oat milk — Closest in texture, good for baking
- 1 cup dairy milk = 1 cup almond milk — Lighter, lower calorie
- 1 cup dairy milk = 1 cup coconut milk — Richer, adds coconut flavor
- 1 cup dairy milk = 1 cup soy milk — High protein, good for all uses
- 1 cup dairy milk = 1 cup cashew milk — Creamy, neutral flavor
- 1 cup dairy milk = 1 cup water + 1 tablespoon yogurt — Emergency substitute
Reason: Plant-based milks have similar liquid content to dairy milk. Flavor and fat content vary by type.

--- CREAM SUBSTITUTIONS ---
- 1 cup heavy cream = 1 cup coconut cream — Vegan, rich and creamy
- 1 cup heavy cream = 3/4 cup whole milk + 1/4 cup melted butter — Similar fat content
- 1 cup heavy cream = 1 cup evaporated milk — Less fat, similar texture
- 1 cup heavy cream = 1 cup full-fat Greek yogurt (for cold applications)
- 1 cup heavy cream = 1 cup cashew cream (blended soaked cashews)
Reason: Heavy cream's main role is adding richness. Match fat content for best results.

--- PANEER SUBSTITUTIONS ---
- Paneer = Tofu (firm) — Best vegan substitute, similar texture
- Paneer = Halloumi cheese — Firmer, saltier, grills well
- Paneer = Ricotta cheese — Softer, crumbler
- Paneer = Queso blanco — Mexican white cheese, doesn't melt
Note: Press tofu well before using as paneer substitute to remove excess moisture.

--- YOGURT SUBSTITUTIONS ---
- 1 cup yogurt = 1 cup sour cream — Almost identical in cooking
- 1 cup yogurt = 1 cup buttermilk — Works in marinades and baked goods
- 1 cup yogurt = 1 cup coconut yogurt — Vegan option
- 1 cup yogurt = 3/4 cup mayonnaise — Use in marinades only
- 1 cup yogurt = 1 cup silken tofu (blended) — Vegan, neutral flavor

--- GHEE SUBSTITUTIONS ---
- 1 tablespoon ghee = 1 tablespoon butter — Most similar in richness
- 1 tablespoon ghee = 1 tablespoon coconut oil — Vegan, high smoke point
- 1 tablespoon ghee = 1 tablespoon clarified butter
- 1 tablespoon ghee = 1 tablespoon olive oil — For lower-heat cooking
Note: Ghee has a higher smoke point than butter, making it better for high-heat cooking.

--- FLOUR SUBSTITUTIONS ---
Gluten-Free Alternatives:
- 1 cup all-purpose flour = 1 cup almond flour (denser baked goods)
- 1 cup all-purpose flour = 1 cup rice flour (lighter texture)
- 1 cup all-purpose flour = 1 cup oat flour (slightly dense, nutty)
- 1 cup all-purpose flour = 7/8 cup chickpea flour (higher protein)
- 1 cup all-purpose flour = 1 cup gluten-free flour blend (closest to regular flour)
Low-Carb Alternatives:
- 1 cup all-purpose flour = 1/4 cup coconut flour (very absorbent, increase liquid)
- 1 cup all-purpose flour = 1 cup almond flour

--- SUGAR SUBSTITUTIONS ---
- 1 cup white sugar = 1 cup coconut sugar (same quantity, lower GI)
- 1 cup white sugar = 3/4 cup maple syrup (reduce other liquid by 1/4 cup)
- 1 cup white sugar = 3/4 cup honey (reduce other liquid by 1/4 cup)
- 1 cup white sugar = 1 cup date sugar
- 1 cup white sugar = 1/2 teaspoon stevia powder
- 1 cup white sugar = 1 cup jaggery (Indian, rich in minerals)
Reason: Natural sweeteners have lower glycemic index. Liquid sweeteners require reducing other liquid in recipe.

--- TOMATO SUBSTITUTIONS ---
- Fresh tomatoes = Canned tomatoes (equal weight) — Convenient, concentrated flavor
- 1 cup fresh tomatoes = 1/2 cup tomato paste + 1/2 cup water
- Tomatoes = Tamarind paste (small amount) — For sourness in Indian cooking
- Tomatoes = Red bell pepper + lemon juice — For color and acidity
- Tomatoes = Sun-dried tomatoes (rehydrated)

--- ONION SUBSTITUTIONS ---
- 1 medium onion = 1/2 cup shallots — Milder, sweeter flavor
- 1 medium onion = 1/2 cup leeks (white part) — Milder
- 1 medium onion = 2 teaspoons onion powder
- 1 medium onion = 1/4 cup chives or green onions (raw dishes)
- For Jain cooking: Use asafoetida (hing) + dried fenugreek leaves

--- GARLIC SUBSTITUTIONS ---
- 1 garlic clove = 1/8 teaspoon garlic powder
- 1 garlic clove = 1/2 teaspoon garlic flakes
- Garlic = Garlic scapes (mild, seasonal)
- For Jain cooking: Use asafoetida (hing) as garlic replacement
Note: Fresh garlic has a sharper, more complex flavor than powder. Increase garlic powder by 50% for similar intensity.

--- OLIVE OIL SUBSTITUTIONS ---
- Olive oil = Avocado oil — Similar health profile, higher smoke point
- Olive oil = Sunflower oil — Neutral flavor, good for frying
- Olive oil = Rice bran oil — Light, high smoke point, good for Indian cooking
- Olive oil = Coconut oil — Adds coconut flavor
Note: For salad dressings, only olive oil provides the authentic Mediterranean flavor.

--- LEMON SUBSTITUTIONS ---
- Lemon juice = Lime juice (1:1 ratio) — Most similar
- Lemon juice = Tamarind paste (1/4 ratio) — More concentrated, tangy
- Lemon juice = White wine vinegar (half the amount)
- Lemon juice = Amchur powder (dry mango) — For Indian recipes
- Lemon zest = Orange zest — Sweeter, less sharp

--- CREAM CHEESE SUBSTITUTIONS ---
- Cream cheese = Mascarpone — Richer, similar texture
- Cream cheese = Full-fat Greek yogurt (strained) — Lighter
- Cream cheese = Silken tofu (blended) — Vegan option
- Cream cheese = Cashew cream — Vegan, rich

--- COCONUT MILK SUBSTITUTIONS ---
- Coconut milk = Cashew cream — Similar richness
- Coconut milk = Oat cream
- Coconut milk = Heavy cream + coconut extract (small amount)
- Coconut milk = Soy milk + coconut extract

--- RICE SUBSTITUTIONS ---
- White rice = Brown rice (longer cooking time, more fiber)
- White rice = Quinoa (higher protein, gluten-free)
- White rice = Cauliflower rice (low-carb)
- White rice = Millet (gluten-free, nutritious)
- Basmati rice = Jasmine rice (slightly different aroma)

--- CHICKPEA SUBSTITUTIONS ---
- Chickpeas = White beans/cannellini beans
- Chickpeas = Lentils (different texture)
- Chickpeas = Black beans
- Chickpeas = Edamame

FOOD SAFETY NOTES:
- Always check for cross-contamination when substituting for allergen ingredients
- Nut-based substitutes should not be used for people with nut allergies
- Test substitutions in small quantities first before making large batches
- Some substitutions may affect cooking time and temperature requirements
